Common Questions
Vehicle Diagnostics FAQ
What does a vehicle diagnostic actually check?
Our full diagnostic scans all available modules in your vehicle — engine, transmission, ABS, airbag, HVAC, and more — and cross-references fault codes with a physical inspection to identify the root cause.
My check engine light is on — do I need a diagnostic?
Yes. A check engine light can indicate anything from a loose gas cap to a serious engine issue. A proper diagnostic is the only way to know for sure — and catching problems early almost always saves money.
Do you do post-collision diagnostics?
Yes — this is one of the most important diagnostics we perform. Modern vehicles have complex sensor networks that can be disrupted in a collision even without visible damage. We check all systems before and after every repair.
How long does a diagnostic take?
A standard diagnostic scan and inspection typically takes 1–2 hours. More complex issues involving multiple systems or intermittent faults may take longer.
